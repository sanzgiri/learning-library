LEVEL 19~10 MIN

The reverse-engineering problem

The Black Swan / Incerto Glossary

Give a physicist an ice cube and ask how it will melt: a well-posed, solvable problem. Show that same physicist a puddle and ask what shape of ice cube produced it: unsolvable — infinitely many starting shapes melt into the same puddle. Forward is easy. Backward is not just harder, it's a different kind of problem.

The idea

"It is easier to predict how an ice cube would melt into a puddle than, looking at a puddle, to guess the shape of the ice cube that may have caused it. This 'inverse problem' makes narrative disciplines and accounts (such as histories) suspicious." Taleb, Incerto Glossary

History, biography, and most "how they did it" writing are puddle-to-ice-cube exercises dressed up as ice-cube-to-puddle ones. The writer has the puddle — the outcome — and reconstructs a single confident-sounding ice cube — the cause — when the honest answer is that countless different starting conditions could have produced the same result. This sharpens the narrative fallacy (lesson 2) into a specific mechanism: it's not just that we prefer stories, it's that the puddle genuinely underdetermines the ice cube, so any story we pick is a guess wearing the costume of a deduction.

Practice

1. Take one "here's how it happened" account you've recently read or heard (a company's rise, a historical turning point, someone's success). List at least two meaningfully different starting conditions ("ice cubes") that could plausibly have melted into the same outcome ("puddle") the account describes.

2. Now do it to your own life: pick one outcome you have a settled explanation for. What's a genuinely different chain of causes that could have produced the identical result?

Something to sit with

If the puddle really does underdetermine the ice cube, is there any way to write history, biography, or a postmortem honestly — or does the inverse problem mean the entire genre is structurally a narrative fallacy generator? What would an honest version of "how it happened" even look like?

Talk it through with your tutor below — they're listening for whether your alternative "ice cubes" are genuinely different causal paths, not just cosmetic variations on the same story.
