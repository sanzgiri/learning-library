# Incerto (Taleb) Resources

## Knowledge

### Cross-cutting
- [Incerto Glossary (PDF)](https://www.fooledbyrandomness.com/glossary.pdf) — Taleb, official glossary on his own site.
  Use for: canonical definitions of Incerto terms. First stop when a lesson needs to define a term precisely instead of paraphrasing from memory. Read in full (not just search-summarized) — confirmed verbatim definitions on file for: lottery-ticket fallacy, ludic fallacy, silent evidence, Mediocristan/Extremistan, problem of induction, **Platonicity** ("the focus on those pure, well-defined, and easily discernible objects... at the cost of ignoring those objects of seemingly messier and less tractable structures"), **Platonic fold**, **confirmation error / Platonic confirmation**, **epistemic arrogance** ("measure the difference between what someone actually knows and how much he thinks he knows"), **empty-suit / expert problem** ("some professionals have no differential abilities from the rest of the population... but are believed to be experts"), **round-trip fallacy** ("the confusion of absence of evidence... for evidence of absence"), **Black Swan ethical problem** ("asymmetry between the rewards of those who prevent and those who cure"), **narrative discipline** (vs. experimental discipline), **Mandelbrotian Gray Swan**. This glossary is the single richest primary source in this file — worth a full re-read before drafting any new lesson, not just a search-engine summary of it.
- [Notebook (numbered notes)](https://www.fooledbyrandomness.com/notebook.htm) — Taleb, personal site.
  Use for: short, dated primary-source notes on specific mechanisms. Relevant numbered entries found so far: #93 (survivorship bias in rare-event probability), #111 (Black Swan as epistemic arrogance), #148 (exposure f(x) vs. the variable x — the convexity distinction), #149 (fragility in nonlinear systems), #153 (bounds vs. point estimates in probability). Cite the specific note number, not just the page.

### Fooled by Randomness — luck vs. skill, survivorship bias, alternative histories, the ludic fallacy
- Book itself (Incerto Book 1) — no free full text found; cite by chapter when grounding a claim beyond the glossary.
- See Glossary and Notebook #93 above. Glossary defines the **ludic fallacy** directly: mistaking the well-posed problems of games/math for the messier uncertainty of real life — including mistaking casino randomness for real-world randomness.
- [Farnam Street: "Nassim Taleb on the Notion of Alternative Histories"](https://fs.blog/nassim-taleb-alternative-history/) — secondary, quotes the book directly.
  Use for: **alternative histories** / **silent evidence** — the idea that you can't judge a decision by its outcome alone, only by the range of outcomes that could plausibly have happened (Taleb's example: $10M from dentistry and $10M from Russian roulette aren't the same bet, even though they spend the same). Verify any direct quote against a print copy before using verbatim in a lesson — this is a secondary compilation, not the book itself.

### The Black Swan — narrative fallacy, rare events, epistemic arrogance
- [Black swan theory (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_swan_theory) — secondary, well-sourced.
  Use for: the precise three-part definition (outlier, extreme impact, retrospective predictability) and the term's origin (Juvenal → the 1697 discovery of actual black swans in Australia). Good for the historical hook in a lesson intro; defer to Taleb's own essays for the argument itself.
- ["The Fourth Quadrant: A Map of the Limits of Statistics"](https://www.edge.org/conversation/nassim_nicholas_taleb-the-fourth-quadrant-a-map-of-the-limits-of-statistics) — Taleb, Edge.org essay.
  Use for: why some domains are unpredictable in principle (fat tails) and what to do about it — the core Black Swan argument in essay form, free and citable.
- ["Ten Principles for a Black-Swan-Robust World"](https://www.edge.org/conversation/nassim_nicholas_taleb-ten-principles-for-a-black-swan-robust-world) — Taleb, Edge.org essay.
  Use for: concrete, numbered principles (fragility, bailouts, skin in the game for decision-makers) — good source for applied/real-life-decision lessons.
- Notebook #111 above.
- **Mediocristan vs. Extremistan** and the **turkey problem** (induction failing under fat tails) are both covered by "The Fourth Quadrant" essay above and the Incerto Glossary — no separate citation needed, but treat these as distinct concepts worth their own lessons rather than folding them into the general Black Swan definition.

### Probability without a spreadsheet — exposure over prediction
- [Statistical Consequences of Fat Tails (free PDF)](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2001.10488) — Taleb, technical Incerto volume.
  Use for: the rigorous version of "why point forecasts fail under fat tails." Advanced/technical — use for grounding, not for lesson prose aimed at a first pass.
- Notebook #148, #153 above.

### Antifragile — fragile/robust/antifragile, stress and dose
- [Antifragility (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antifragility) — secondary, well-sourced.
  Use for: the crisp one-line definition and the resilient-vs-antifragile contrast ("the resilient resists shocks and stays the same; the antifragile gets better"), useful for a lesson's opening hook. Confirmed: this article does *not* cover via negativa or the barbell — don't cite it for those.
- ["Understanding Is a Poor Substitute for Convexity (Antifragility)"](https://www.edge.org/conversation/nassim_nicholas_taleb-understanding-is-a-poor-substitute-for-convexity-antifragility) — Taleb, Edge.org essay.
  Use for: the core fragile/robust/antifragile distinction and why convexity matters more than "understanding," in Taleb's own words.
- Notebook #148, #149 above.
- [An Expert Called Lindy](https://medium.com/incerto/an-expert-called-lindy-fdb30f146eaf) — Taleb, essay on Medium (Incerto), mirrored at [nassimtaleb.org](https://nassimtaleb.org/2017/03/medium-post-expert-called-lindy/).
  Use for: the **Lindy effect** — for non-perishable things (ideas, books, technologies, institutions), the longer something has survived, the longer its remaining life expectancy. Strong free primary source, directly citable.
- **Iatrogenics / naive interventionism** (harm caused by well-intentioned intervention, "caused by the healer") and **domain dependence** (understanding a pattern in one context but failing to recognize it in another) are both introduced in the *Antifragile* book itself. **Gap**: no free standalone Taleb essay found for either — cite by chapter, not URL, until one turns up.

### Via negativa & the barbell strategy
- Book itself (*Antifragile*, the barbell-strategy chapter) — no free excerpt found; cite by chapter, not URL, when grounding this topic.
- **Gap**: no free primary essay found specifically on via negativa/barbell. Treat book-chapter citation as the source of truth until one turns up.

### Optionality & convexity
- Same Edge.org convexity essay as Antifragile, above — Taleb treats convexity and antifragility as the same mechanism.
- Notebook #148 above.

### Skin in the game
- ["The Intellectual Yet Idiot"](https://nassimtaleb.org/2016/09/intellectual-yet-idiot/) — Taleb, excerpted chapter from *Skin in the Game*, on his own site.
  Use for: the accountability/asymmetry argument in full, unabridged, in Taleb's own words — strongest primary source found for this topic.
- "Ten Principles for a Black-Swan-Robust World" above also touches skin-in-the-game (bonuses, bailouts, accountability of decision-makers).
- [The Most Intolerant Wins: The Dictatorship of the Small Minority](https://medium.com/incerto/the-most-intolerant-wins-the-dictatorship-of-the-small-minority-3f1f83ce4e15) — Taleb, essay on Medium (Incerto).
  Use for: **minority rule** — a small, intransigent, asymmetric minority (e.g. those who will only eat kosher/halal, or refuse an allergen) can force the whole population to comply, because the majority's preference is flexible and the minority's isn't. Verified quote: "A Kosher (or halal) eater will never eat nonkosher (or nonhalal) food, but a nonkosher eater isn't banned from eating kosher."

### Ergodicity & the logic of risk taking
- [The Logic of Risk Taking](https://medium.com/incerto/the-logic-of-risk-taking-107bf41029d3) — Taleb, essay on Medium (Incerto), mirrored at [nassimtaleb.org](https://nassimtaleb.org/2017/08/medium-logic-risk-taking/). Taleb calls it "a central chapter that crystallizes all my work."
  Use for: **ergodicity** — the difference between 100 people gambling once (ensemble average) and one person gambling 100 times (time average), and why risk of ruin breaks the equivalence between them. Strong free primary source, directly citable. Note: do *not* use the popular "never cross a river that is on average four feet deep" line as a Taleb quote — it's usually attributed to Milton Friedman, not Taleb; use Taleb's own casino example instead.
- [Ergodicity Economics](https://ergodicityeconomics.com/) and [The Ergodicity Problem in Economics (Nature Physics, 2019)](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41567-019-0732-0) — Ole Peters (see Blogs section below).
  Use for: the formal/academic framing of the same argument, if a lesson needs to go past the intuitive casino example.

### The Bed of Procrustes — aphorisms
- Book itself (Incerto Book 3, standalone aphorisms) — no free full text; this book is aphoristic by design, so cite individual aphorisms with quotation marks and note they're compressed, deliberately overstated one-liners, not arguments to be taken too literally.
- [Farnam Street: "The Bed of Procrustes: 20 Aphorisms from Nassim Taleb"](https://fs.blog/the-bed-of-procrustes/) — secondary compilation.
  Use for: sourcing candidate aphorisms for a lesson. Cross-check wording against a second compilation (e.g. Goodreads' quotes page) before treating any single aphorism as verbatim, since these circulate widely and get misquoted.

## Blogs

- [Farnam Street (fs.blog)](https://fs.blog/) — secondary, well-established mental-models blog.
  Already used above as a secondary source for alternative histories and Bed of Procrustes aphorisms. Reliable for orientation and for compiling quotes, but always treat as secondary — verify anything load-bearing against a primary Taleb source or the book itself.
- [Ergodicity Economics (ergodicityeconomics.com)](https://ergodicityeconomics.com/) — Ole Peters, physicist and Taleb's collaborator on the ergodicity/time-average argument.
  Use for: **ergodicity** — the research programme's own site, with posts, lecture notes, and publications on why time averages diverge from ensemble averages under risk of ruin. Not Taleb's own writing, but the co-originator of the argument Taleb popularized; strong grounding for that topic specifically. See also the peer-reviewed paper ["The Ergodicity Problem in Economics" (Nature Physics, 2019)](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41567-019-0732-0), co-authored by Peters.

## Wisdom (Communities)

- [Real World Risk Institute (realworldrisk.com)](https://realworldrisk.com/) — Taleb's own teaching institute, founded 2015.
  This is genuine wisdom infrastructure, not just a knowledge source: a place to test risk-taking judgment against real practitioners, not just read about it. Paid ($2,995 for the flagship workshop as of the 2025 cohort; scholarships covering up to 65% are advertised) — note the cost as a real constraint before recommending it.
- **Reddit — checked, not found.** I ran multiple targeted searches (direct subreddit name, "site:reddit.com," general "is there a Taleb subreddit" queries) and found no dedicated, verified, active Taleb-focused subreddit. One earlier search surfaced a plausible-sounding claim with no actual link behind it — unreliable, not cited. General subreddits (r/quant, r/philosophy, r/investing) likely have scattered threads mentioning his work, but I have no evidence any of them are high-signal or Taleb-specific communities, so I'm not recommending them. If you find one yourself, it's easy to add here.

## Gaps

- No free full text for the Incerto books themselves (copyrighted) — any lesson content grounded only in the books, not the free essays above, should cite chapter/section, not a URL.
- Via negativa / barbell strategy, iatrogenics / naive interventionism, and domain dependence all have no free Taleb essay found yet — thinnest-sourced topics in this file. All three rest on book-chapter citation only.
- The Bed of Procrustes is aphoristic by construction — no argument to cite, just one-liners. Treat any single aphorism as illustrative, not as proof of a claim.
- No verified Reddit (or other online forum) community found — see Wisdom section above. Real World Risk Institute fills the wisdom role but is paid, which is itself a constraint worth surfacing to the user before recommending it.
